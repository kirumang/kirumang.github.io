🔭 Research Landscape

Today's batch of twenty papers reveals three dominant currents shaping robotics research. The most striking is a growing crisis of measurement: multiple papers argue that the field's current evaluation tools are systematically misleading. "From Inference to Embodied Efficiency" demonstrates that standard VLA efficiency metrics (FLOPs, parameter counts, throughput) fail to predict actual robot execution behavior, while "Not All Features Are Created Equal" uses mechanistic probing across six VLA models to show that visual pathways dominate action generation — yet this is invisible to task-success metrics. DROID-SLAM in the Wild similarly challenges the static-scene assumption that pervades SLAM evaluation. Taken together, these papers sound a clear warning: the robotics field needs richer, embodied evaluation frameworks before claims about model superiority can be trusted.

A second dominant thread is the push toward tactile and contact-aware manipulation grounded in large-scale data. OmniVTA introduces a dataset of 21,000+ visuo-tactile trajectories organized around six physics-grounded interaction patterns, coupling it with a world-model-based controller that predicts contact evolution rather than reacting after the fact. ViTac-Tracing addresses the deformable object tracing problem with a unified visual-tactile model that eliminates object-specific engineering. Both papers share the insight that tactile data, used predictively rather than as passive observation, unlocks manipulation capabilities that vision alone cannot provide. This is complemented by V-Dreamer's approach of using video generation models as motion priors to automate training data synthesis — suggesting a near-future where large vision-language models and large tactile datasets converge into comprehensive manipulation systems.

The third current cuts across categories: real-time, deployable intelligence for physically demanding tasks. FASTER rethinks flow-based VLA sampling to compress reaction latency by 10× without sacrificing trajectory quality, validated on a table tennis task requiring sub-100ms reaction. The ADMM-distributed MPC framework reduces multi-robot planning time by 51% while preserving formal safety guarantees. ABD-Net embeds rigid-body dynamics structure into policy networks to improve sample efficiency and generalization. Even hardware papers like quadrupedal skating and the airdrop sensor mechanism are driven by the same concern: achieving high performance with minimal computational overhead. The field appears to be transitioning from demonstrating that complex behaviors are possible to demanding that they be fast, safe, and deployable.

From Inference Efficiency to Embodied Efficiency: Revisiting Efficiency Metrics for Vision-Language-Action Models

2026-03-19cs.LGcs.ROChaojian Li (h=19)

Zhuofan Li, Hongkun Yang, Zhenyang Chen, Yangxuan Chen, Yingyan

Core Contributions
  • ► Challenges the foundational assumption of VLA efficiency research: standard ML metrics (FLOPs, parameters, tokens/sec) systematically fail to predict real-world robotic execution behavior.
  • ► Defines four "embodied efficiency" metrics — task completion time, trajectory smoothness, cumulative joint rotation, and motion energy — that reveal policy differences invisible to conventional benchmarks.
  • ► Counter-intuitive finding: methods that reduce computation under conventional metrics often increase end-to-end execution cost or degrade motion quality, even while maintaining task success rates.
  • ► Common adaptation methods (in-context prompting, supervised fine-tuning) show only mild, metric-specific improvements — gains in one embodied metric come at the cost of others.
  • ► Provides a more complete evaluation framework that should replace or supplement standard benchmarks in VLA research, enabling fairer and more practically relevant model comparisons.
Show abstract

We find that the prevailing notion of efficiency in VLA research — parameters, FLOPs, token decoding throughput — does not reflect actual performance on robotic platforms. Efficiency in real-world execution is determined by embodied behaviors such as task completion time, trajectory smoothness, cumulative joint rotation, and motion energy. Methods that reduce computation under conventional metrics often increase end-to-end execution cost or degrade motion quality. Common adaptation methods such as in-context prompting or supervised fine-tuning show only mild and metric-specific improvements in embodied efficiency.

FASTER: Rethinking Real-Time Flow VLAs

2026-03-19cs.ROcs.CVHengshuang Zhao (h=9)

Yuxiang Lu, Zhe Liu, Xianzhe Fan, Zhenya Yang, Jinghua Hou

Core Contributions
  • ► Identifies a structural problem in flow-based VLA inference: constant sampling schedules force all denoising steps to complete before any motion starts — creating an avoidable reaction latency bottleneck.
  • ► Derives that reaction time follows a uniform distribution jointly determined by Time to First Action (TTFA) and execution horizon — a formal characterization that clarifies what can be optimized.
  • ► Horizon-Aware Schedule adaptively prioritizes near-term actions during flow sampling, compressing the denoising of the immediate reaction from full steps to a single step (10x compression) while preserving long-horizon quality.
  • ► Streaming client-server pipeline further reduces effective reaction latency, enabling real-time responsiveness even on consumer-grade GPUs — democratizing VLA deployment beyond research clusters.
  • ► Validated on a highly dynamic table tennis task requiring sub-100ms reaction, proving that FASTER unlocks real-time responsiveness for generalist policies in physically demanding scenarios.
Show abstract

Real-time execution is crucial for deploying VLA models physically. Existing asynchronous inference methods optimize trajectory smoothness but neglect reaction latency. Reaction time follows a uniform distribution determined by Time to First Action (TTFA) and execution horizon. Constant schedules in flow-based VLAs force all sampling steps to complete before movement starts. FASTER's Horizon-Aware Schedule adaptively prioritizes near-term actions, compressing denoising of the immediate reaction by tenfold into a single step while preserving long-horizon trajectory quality. Validated on a highly dynamic table tennis task.

2026-03-19cs.ROXijia Zhao (h=7)

Bryce Grant, Xijia Zhao, Peng Wang

Core Contributions

We apply activation injection, sparse autoencoders (SAEs), and linear probes to six VLA models (80M–7B parameters) across 394,000+ rollout episodes on four benchmarks. The visual pathway dominates action generation: injecting baseline activations into null-prompt episodes recovers near-identical behavior (99.8% of X-VLA episodes align with source trajectory). Language sensitivity depends on task structure: when visual context uniquely specifies the task, language is ignored; when multiple goals share a scene, language becomes essential (94%→10% under wrong prompts). ViTac-Tracing: Visual-Tactile Imitation Learning of Deformable Object Tracing

2026-03-19cs.ROY. Demiris (h=46)

Yongqiang Zhao, Haining Luo, Yupeng Wang, Emmanouil Spyrakos Papastavridis, Yiannis Demiris

Core Contributions
  • ► A single unified model handles both 1D (ropes, wires) and 2D (cloth, fabric) deformable object tracing — prior work required object-specific pipelines or brittle sim-to-real transfer that fails in real-world conditions.
  • ► Integrates tactile sensing into a low-cost teleoperation system, enabling data collection that captures both visual and tactile modalities simultaneously — removing the expensive specialized hardware typically required for tactile demonstration.
  • ► Weighted loss emphasizes actions that maintain contact near the center of the tactile image, providing fine-grained finger positioning that vision alone cannot achieve.

Show abstract

Deformable objects often appear in unstructured configurations. Tracing deformable objects helps bringing them into extended states and facilitating the downstream manipulation tasks. Due to the requirements for object-specific modeling or sim-to-real transfer, existing tracing methods either lack generalizability across different categories of deformable objects or struggle to complete tasks reliably in the real world. To address this, we propose a novel visual-tactile imitation learning method to achieve one-dimensional (1D) and two-dimensional (2D) deformable object tracing with a unified model. Our method is designed from both local and global perspectives based on visual and tactile sensing. Globally, we propose a tracing task loss that helps the policy to regulate task progression. On the hardware side, to compensate for the limited features extracted from visual information, we integrate tactile sensing into a low-cost teleoperation system. Extensive ablation and comparative experiments on diverse 1D and 2D deformable objects demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach, achieving an average success rate of 80% on seen objects and 65% on unseen objects.

Articulated-Body Dynamics Network: Dynamics-Grounded Prior for Robot Learning

2026-03-19cs.ROJosiah P. Hanna (h=22)

Sangwoo Shin, Kunzhao Ren, Xiaobin Xiong, Josiah Hanna

Core Contributions
  • ► Prior physics-informed policy networks use kinematic structure (joint connectivity) as an inductive bias, but ignore dynamics — how inertia and forces propagate through the body. ABD-Net fills this gap.
  • ► Adapts the Articulated Body Algorithm's child-to-parent inertia propagation into a learnable GNN, replacing physical quantities with learned parameters while preserving the tree-structured aggregation pattern.
  • ► Achieves greater sample efficiency and stronger generalization to dynamics shifts compared to both transformer and GNN baselines across humanoid, quadruped, and hopper morphologies.
  • ► Validates sim-to-real transfer on Unitree G1 (humanoid) and Go2 (quadruped) at real-time inference rates — demonstrating practical deployment potential beyond simulation.
  • ► Provides a template for using classical rigid-body dynamics algorithms (like the ABA) as architectural blueprints for more data-efficient neural policy networks.
Show abstract

Recent work in reinforcement learning has shown that incorporating structural priors for articulated robots — such as link connectivity — into policy networks improves learning efficiency. However, dynamics properties, despite their fundamental role in determining how forces and motion propagate through the body, remain largely underexplored as an inductive bias for policy learning. We present ABD-Net, a novel graph neural network architecture grounded in the computational structure of forward dynamics. We adapt the inertia propagation mechanism from the Articulated Body Algorithm, systematically aggregating inertial quantities from child to parent links in a tree-structured manner, while replacing physical quantities with learnable parameters. Through experiments with simulated humanoid, quadruped, and hopper robots, our approach demonstrates increased sample efficiency and generalization to dynamics shifts compared to transformer-based and GNN baselines. We validate the learned policy on real Unitree G1 and Go2 robots with real-time inference.

REST: Receding Horizon Explorative Steiner Tree for Zero-Shot Object-Goal Navigation

2026-03-19cs.ROcs.AIcs.CVMaani Ghaffari (h=17)

Shuqi Xiao, Maani Ghaffari, Chengzhong Xu, Hui Kong

Core Contributions
  • ► Identifies a previously overlooked gap in zero-shot object-goal navigation: existing methods carefully design scene understanding (belief) and decision-making (policy) but treat subgoal candidates (options) as isolated waypoints — losing all en-route information value.
  • ► Key insight: the option space should be a tree of paths, not individual destinations — full paths expose en-route information gain that destination-only scoring systematically misses.
  • ► Tree structure enables coarse-to-fine LLM reasoning: dismiss or pursue entire branches before examining leaves, compressing the exponential path-space into a tractable hierarchy.
  • ► Fully training-free: builds open-vocabulary 3D maps from online RGB-D and uses chain-of-thought LLM reasoning for path selection — no task-specific fine-tuning needed.
  • ► Consistently ranks among top methods on Gibson, HM3D, and HSSD benchmarks while achieving best or second-best path efficiency — demonstrating a favorable efficiency-success tradeoff.
Show abstract

Zero-shot object-goal navigation (ZSON) requires navigating unknown environments without task-specific training. Prior hierarchical solutions invest in scene understanding and high-level decision-making but overlook option design — subgoal candidates proposed from evolving belief. Options are reduced to isolated waypoints scored independently, hiding en-route information gain. REST represents the option space as a tree of paths: full paths expose en-route information gain that destination-only scoring neglects; a tree enables coarse-to-fine LLM reasoning. REST builds an open-vocabulary 3D map from RGB-D streams, grows an agent-centric tree of safe paths, and selects the next-best path through chain-of-thought LLM reasoning. REST consistently ranks among top methods on Gibson, HM3D, and HSSD while achieving best or second-best path efficiency.

2026-03-19cs.ROmath.OCK. Hamed (h=19)

Yicheng Zeng, Ruturaj S. Sambhus, Basit Muhammad Imran, Jeeseop Kim, Vittorio Pastore

Core Contributions
  • ► Safety-critical multi-robot legged control typically requires centralized MPC that cannot scale; this work achieves full decentralization with formal safety guarantees preserved through CBF constraints.
  • ► CBF constraints create inter-agent coupling that breaks standard ADMM decomposition — the key contribution is a novel node-edge splitting with consensus constraints that resolves this while enabling parallel QP solving.
  • ► Each agent solves only local QPs using neighbor-to-neighbor communication — symmetric computational load across agents, no central coordinator, and no global model of the environment needed.
  • ► Reduces average per-cycle planning time by up to 51% vs. centralized MPC in the 4-agent case, enabling real-time decentralized implementation.
  • ► Validated on physical Unitree Go2 robots navigating rough terrain under external disturbances, demonstrating the framework's practical robustness beyond simulated results.
Show abstract

This paper proposes a fully decentralized MPC framework with CBF constraints for safety-critical trajectory planning in multi-robot legged systems. CBF constraints introduce inter-agent coupling, preventing direct decomposition via ADMM. We introduce a node-edge splitting formulation with consensus constraints that decomposes the global problem into independent node-local and edge-local QPs solvable in parallel with only neighbor-to-neighbor communication. The proposed framework reduces average per-cycle planning time by up to 51% in the four-agent case while preserving safety and dynamic feasibility. Validated on Unitree Go2 robots navigating rough terrain with external disturbances.
